## 1. Michaelsen MK et al. (2025) — GLP-1 RA in Nondiabetic Ischemic Stroke

**Citation**: Michaelsen MK, Drasbek KR, Valentin JB, et al. GLP-1 Receptor Agonists as Treatment of Nondiabetic Ischemic Stroke: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis. *Stroke*. 2025;57(2):415-437.
**DOI**: [10.1161/STROKEAHA.125.053075](https://doi.org/10.1161/STROKEAHA.125.053075)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Design | Systematic review and meta-analysis (preclinical + clinical) |
| N | 35 studies (31 preclinical animal models, 4 clinical trials) |
| Intervention | GLP-1 RA (9 different therapies) in nondiabetic stroke |
| Comparator | Vehicle/placebo |
| Primary outcome | Functional outcome, infarct volume |
| Key findings | GLP-1 RA improved functional outcome and markedly reduced infarct volume in preclinical models. Proposed mechanisms: reduced oxidative stress, hypoxia-triggered cell death, and inflammatory response. However, recent RCTs have **not** shown significant effect on stroke incidence or neurological recovery in nondiabetic patients. |
| Limitations | Most evidence is preclinical; only 4 clinical studies; nondiabetic population (not T2DM) |
| Relevance | Provides mechanistic rationale for neuroprotection but highlights the translational gap from bench to bedside |

## 2. Hasebe M et al. (2026) — GLP-1 RA and CV Outcomes by Race

**Citation**: Hasebe M, Su CY, Kamido H, Yabe D, Yoshiji S. GLP-1 receptor agonists and cardiovascular outcomes in Asian, Black or African American, and White populations: An updated meta-analysis including the SOUL trial. *Diabetes Obes Metab*. 2026;28(4):2806-2814.
**DOI**: [10.1111/dom.70458](https://doi.org/10.1111/dom.70458)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Design | Updated meta-analysis of RCTs with race stratification |
| N | 74,703 participants across 9 RCTs (including SOUL trial) |
| Intervention | GLP-1 RA |
| Comparator | Placebo |
| Primary outcome | 3-point MACE (CV death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke) |
| Key findings | Asian: HR 0.73 (95% CI 0.63-0.85, p<0.001); White: HR 0.86 (0.81-0.91, p<0.001); Black/AA: HR 0.88 (0.67-1.15, p=0.34, NS). Significantly greater relative risk reduction in Asian vs. White populations (RHR 0.84, 0.71-0.98, p=0.027). |
| Limitations | Black/AA population underpowered (n=4,036 vs. 62,503 White); race-stratified stroke-specific data not reported separately |
| Relevance | Demonstrates MACE reduction across racial groups with important equity implications for GLP-1 RA prescribing |

## 4. Shokravi A et al. (2026) — Tirzepatide vs GLP-1 RA Network Meta-Analysis

**Citation**: Shokravi A, Seth J, Mancini GBJ. Comparative efficacy of tirzepatide and GLP-1 RA on cardiovascular outcomes in patients with type 2 diabetes: a systematic review and network meta-analysis. *Cardiovasc Diabetol*. 2026;25(1).

| N | 11 RCTs (10 GLP-1 RA trials + SURPASS-CVOT) |
| Intervention | Tirzepatide (dual GIP/GLP-1 RA) vs. individual GLP-1 RAs |

## 6. Ahmed AAO et al. (2025) — SGLT2i vs GLP-1 RA for MACE

**Citation**: Ahmed AAO, Ahmed OAO, Ahmed RAO, et al. SGLT2 inhibitors versus GLP-1 receptor agonists for major adverse cardiovascular events in type 2 diabetes: a systematic review and meta-analysis of RCTs. *BMC Cardiovasc Disord*. 2025;26(1):88.
**DOI**: [10.1186/s12872-025-05455-4](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12872-025-05455-4)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Design | Systematic review and meta-analysis with indirect treatment comparison (Bucher method) |
| N | 99,261 participants across 12 RCTs (5 SGLT2i, 7 GLP-1 RA) |
| Intervention | SGLT2 inhibitors vs. GLP-1 RA |
| Comparator | Placebo (with indirect comparison between drug classes) |
| Primary outcome | 3-point MACE |
| Key findings | **GLP-1 RA vs. placebo**: MACE HR 0.82 (0.78-0.87, p<0.00001). **SGLT2i vs. placebo**: MACE HR 0.89 (0.84-0.95, p=0.0003). Indirect comparison: GLP-1 RA showed nominally greater MACE reduction (HR 1.09, 1.00-1.18, p=0.0497). SGLT2i superior for HF hospitalization (HR 0.68) and renal outcomes (HR 0.69). |
| Limitations | Indirect comparison only (no head-to-head RCT data); different trial populations; MACE composite does not separate stroke from MI |
| Relevance | Positions GLP-1 RA as potentially superior to SGLT2i for atherosclerotic events (including stroke) while SGLT2i excel for HF/renal; supports complementary use |
